Soil Requirements



Moong grows well in well-drained, sandy loam soils with good organic matter. The soil pH should be between 6.0 and 7.5 for optimal growth. It's important to ensure that the soil is well-drained to prevent waterlogging.

Planting Depth and Spacing



Moong seeds should be planted at a depth of about 3 to 5 centimeters with row spacing ranging from 15 to 30 centimeters. Proper spacing allows each plant to have enough room to grow and access sunlight and nutrients effectively.

Weed Management



Effective weed management is crucial for moong cultivation to reduce competition for nutrients and water. Methods such as manual weeding, mulching, and the use of herbicides can help control weeds and ensure optimal growth of the crop.

Market Considerations



Understanding market demand and trends is crucial for successful moong cultivation. Factors such as consumer preferences, market prices, and export opportunities should be considered when planning moong production to maximize profitability.
